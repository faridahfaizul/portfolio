Artliys

This page contains the case study of Artliys Project which includes the project overview, user research, user personas, wireframes, prototypes, usability studies, mockups and take away of the project.

Prototype Link

Project Overview

Artliys is the design of mobile application that I did during completing the Google UX Design Certificate. It's main highlight is the audio feature for users to use when they are inside the gallery.

Artliys is a mobile application that provides audio feature when users are inside the galleries. This features can help galleries to share more information to the users and help users with reading disabilities. The application also allows users to control the audio playback. The goal of this project is to design a mobile app that can help users to get more information easily on artworks that is available in the art gallery with the help of audio feature.

Project Duration

May 2023

Role & Responsibilities

UX designer designing an app for Artliys from conception to delivery.

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

User Research

I conducted user research for this project by using the sample bios provided. Empathy maps has helped in understanding the users and filling in the gap between what I thought is sufficient for the users and what the users actually need.

The user research confirmed that users are having trouble visiting the art galleries during busy days or peak times, but also helped to revealed that there are other types of users that could find the application feature very helpful to support their disabilities. This helped me to view how different people view each situation differently depending their point of view and how crucial this process is to produce a good experience to them. User research also helped to gain more insights on what other challenges they face and how it can be resolved.

User Persona

Starting the Design

I started on my design by sketching the big picture and close-up storyboard to have a visual representation of the user's interaction with the product. After getting a clear view on the product's direction, wireframes were sketched as the initial design. Taking some time to draft each screen of the app on paper to ensure that all of the elements are well-suited to address the user’s pain points. After finalizing the wireframes, I then transform the paper wireframe to digital wireframe using Figma.

Usability Studies

Two rounds of usability studies was conducted for this project. Findings from the first user study guides me to design the mobile app from wireframes to mockups while the second study was used to improve the high-fidelity prototype that needed more refining. The goal of the studies were to determine if the users faced any difficulties while navigating through the app. Figure out if the information and details was sufficient for users and if the information was presented in a way that all users can access to it.

  • Unmoderated usability study
  • Location: Remote (each participant went through the usability study in their own home)
  • Length: Each session will last 10 to 20 minutes, based on a list of prompts, with SUS and NPS following the study

Round 1 Findings

  • Users need a better representation for the icons used
  • Users need a clearer direction on getting the artwork list
  • Users need a more clearer direction that does not require the user to spend time to understand the functionality

Round 2 Findings

  • Cannot get the details of the artworks from “Audio” tab
  • Users want to browse around the app while listening to the audio

It was observed from the first usability study that users felt confused and frustrated to start the task given. The many options available at the homepage to view the artworks makes users to choose an option that is not easy for the to get the full list. Therefore, I have removed some method to view and adjust the layout to make it more understandable for users. Common icons were also used so that users immediately knows the functionality of it.

The second usability study revealed user’s frustration with the audio playback UI. Users complained about not being able to browse around the app while listening to the audio. I made the option for users to minimize the audio playback popup to allow the users to keep using the app and having a minimized view of the audio being played in the background.

Accessibility considerations

  • Provided access to users who experienced reading disabilities by having the audio playback UI to control the audio feature.
  • Use a clear icon to help make user’s navigation easier.
  • Provide images and guides for users to locate the artworks and galleries.

Takeaways

Impact:

  • The app is very easy to understand and navigate, making users feel welcomed.
  • One quote from peer feedback: “I like the distinction between gallery audio tour and individual artwork audio. This is easy to understand and helps organize a lot of different tours. I am very impressed and a little jealous! The overall feeling is very cohesive and inviting, and the interactions are easy to follow. Great work.”

What I learned:

  • While designing the Artliys app, I discovered that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of many iterative processes. Usability studies and peer feedbacks gives a high influence during each iteration being made of the app’s designs.

Next Steps

  • Conduct more user research to determine any new areas that are needed to fulfil the user’s need.
  • Conduct another round of usability study to validate whether the pain points have been effectively addressed.
  • Update the high-fidelity prototype to have a more cleaner user flows.
