Clicom

This page contains the case study of Clicom Project which includes the project overview, user research, user personas, wireframes, prototypes, usability studies, mockups and take away of the project.

Prototype Link

Project Overview

Clicom is the final project that was completed during my time enrolling in Google UX Design Certificate. Clicom is a Malaysia-based community service mobile app and responsive website focuses on advertising local community services. The community needs a platform that helps people acknowledging the current activities that are happening in town.

Clicom’s primary target users include college students and adults who wanted to be involved in volunteering to their local community services. The goal of this project is to design a platform that will become a hub for advertising local community services to allow communities to stay alert on upcoming programs.

Project Duration

May - June 2023

Role & Responsibilities

UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery.

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

User Research

I conducted an early interview earlier in the project to understand user’s opinion and knowledge on local community services. Most of the interview participants stated that local community services does not advertise their activities publicly, making them not aware on current activities conducted in the community. The feedback received through user research made it clear that some of the users are actually interested in joining and taking part in their community services but are not aware on upcoming events and activities, thus making these programs are not receiving good response.

User Persona

Based on my research, I concluded that there were only 1 key user type that my design tried to solve problems for. Ahmad is a full time student who needs latest information regarding local community services because they does not want to miss out some great opportunities especially programs that interests them.

Starting the Design

I started ideating on my design by doing 'Crazy Eights Exercise', a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas on the user flow and improving the gaps identified in the competitive audit. My focus during this process was advertising local community services and steps for users to join. After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes, I created the initial designs for the Clicom Community Service app. These designs focused on delivering important information about local community services to the users.

Usability Studies

A round of usability studies was conducted for this project as the initial testing. Findings from the user study helps to guide me throughout the designing process for the responsive website. The main goal of the study were to determine if the flow was clear enough for users to navigate through the app.

  • Unmoderated usability study
  • Location: Remote (each participant went through the usability study in their own home)
  • Length: Each session will last 10 to 20 minutes, based on a list of prompts

Findings

  • Users had difficulties finding the confirmation alert.
  • Users want to view the list of community services that they signed up for.
  • Users wants to view the details of the community services in a hierarchical order.

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes at the details page, specifically focusing on how I present the information that will ease users to grasps all the important details of the community service. The information is presented in a more hierarchical order, easing the users to find the information needed.

Additional design changes included adding an a complete icon at the Completion page and adding a button to navigate users to their profile to view the lists of community services they joined and signed up for.

Accessibility considerations

  • Use a clear labels and icons to help make user’s navigation easier in which the elements can also be read by screen readers.
  • Homepage is designed to help users access all the functionalities such as user profile, summary of joined activities and latest community services in town without navigating to other pages.
  • Provide images and map guides for users to locate the location where the community services will be held.

Sitemap

With the app designs completed, I started work on designing the responsive website. I used the Clicom sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen’s design to ensure a cohesive and consistent experience across devices.

Responsive Designs

The designs for screen size variation included mobile, tablet and desktop. I optimized the designs to fit specific user needs of each device and screen size.

Takeaways

Impact:

  • Users shared that the app made local community services much easier to be acknowledged. The user research helped me to understand on the user's pain points and what it needed to ensure the end product satisfies the user.

What I learned:

  • Throughout the user research, I learned that providing comprehensive and accurate information and creating a simple but user-friendly interface are crucial for encouraging the community service experience. Community services act should be made easy and simple for users to sign up to ensure the anticipation remains the same throughout the process.

Next Steps

  • Conduct more user research to determine any new areas that are needed to fulfil the user’s need and validate whether the pain points have been effectively addressed.
  • Conduct research on how successful the app is in reaching the goal to increase the participants of local community services.
  • Explore partnerships and collaborations with community centers, educational institutions and organizations to promote the app and integrate it into their own use.
