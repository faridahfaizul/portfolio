Anne's Bakery

This page contains the case study of Anne's Bakery Project which includes the project overview, user research, user personas, wireframes, prototypes, usability studies, mockups and take away of the project.

Prototype Link

Project Overview

Anne's Bakery is a design for online bakery shopping website. This project was done during the completing of Google UX Design Certificate.

Anne's Bakery website is a responsive website designed to help users to purchase items in the shop much easier, whether they choose to dine-in or take away their orders. With the goal of connecting users with the love for coffees and pastries to the bakery itself, Anne's Bakery ordering website ensures that users can easily create their orders with just a few taps.

Project Duration

May 2023

Role & Responsibilities

UX designer designing an app for Artliys from conception to delivery.

Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, iterating on designs, determining information architecture, and responsive design.

User Research

I conducted an interview earlier in the project to understand user’s opinion on local bakeries near them. One of the participants stated that during their visit to the bakery, sometimes, most of their items were already sold out. They stated their frustration of wasting their going to an empty store. Another participant stated that it is difficult to get a freshly baked menus since they don't have enough time to visit any bakery due to their work and had to encounter traffic during peak hours.

Understanding the Problem

User Persona

Based on my research, I concluded that there were 2 key user type that my design tried to solve problems for. I decided to focus on Persona 1 but I believe the solution I am creating can solve both of the personas main pain points which is making an order in advanced.

User Journey Map

Mapping Kathy’s user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have the ability to make online orders from the bakery.

Sitemap

I started work on designing the responsive website by using sitemap where you provide information about the pages and the relationships between them. It is crucial to know the user flow and what to expect upon clicking or opening a certain page. A sitemap is useful to organize the information in each pages.

Usability Studies

A round of usability studies was conducted for this project as the initial testing. Findings from the user study helps to guide me throughout the designing process for the responsive website. The main goal of the study were to determine if the flow was clear enough for users to navigate through the app.

  • Unmoderated usability study
  • Location: Remote (each participant went through the usability study in their own home)
  • Length: Each session will last 10 to 20 minutes, based on a list of prompts

Findings

  • Users want to view the status of their order.
  • Users thinks the checkout process is easy and in good flow.
  • Users want confirmation about their information when checking out.
  • User thinks the hero image should get users' attention and clear of their business

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design which highlights the main part upon opening the website which is the hero image. focusing on the business type.

Main changes that has been made after the usability testing is the status page. Users wanted to see the status of their order in order to collect them. Adding a new page to display their order status could help them to know the current process of preparing their order and the estimated time to complete.

Accessibility considerations

  • Use headings with different size text to help user’s navigation easier and having clear hierarchy.
  • Use clear labels to help users access all the functionalities such as shop, about, contacts and user profile.
  • Using a soft and color scheme so that users feel relaxed while browsing and shopping.

Takeaways

Impact:

  • The user research plays a major role in understanding the user's need and pain points. This is important to know what is required to ensure that the end product meet their needs.

What I learned:

  • While designing the website, I realized that we did not really know what the user want, but with user research we can get much better understanding and what are the user's pain points in order to solve their frustrations. Some users needed a visually appealing website with a lot of creativity but some just needed their product to do complete its main purpose and functionality. It is important to understand the user clearly and best to avoid any assumptions on what we think works best for them.

Next Steps

  • Conduct a follow-up usability testing to determine any areas that needed update.
  • Identify any new areas that are needed to add in order to fulfil the user’s need.
  • Conduct research on how successful the website in reaching the it's goal.
